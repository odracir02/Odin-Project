Serrano ham croquettes


Description:

Serrano ham croquettes are a popular Spanish tapa made from a creamy béchamel sauce mixed with small pieces of cured Serrano ham. The mixture is then shaped into small cylinders or balls, coated in breadcrumbs, and deep-fried until crispy on the outside and creamy on the inside. The result is a delicious combination of salty, savory ham and rich, smooth béchamel that melts in your mouth. Serrano ham croquettes are a classic dish in Spanish cuisine and are often served as an appetizer or snack with a cold beer or glass of wine.


Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped Serrano ham
  • Salt, black pepper, and nutmeg to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 cup of breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Steps:

  1. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, stirring occasionally.

  2. Add the flour and whisk until well combined. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.

  3. Gradually add the milk while whisking, making sure there are no lumps. Cook for 10-12 minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and pulls away from the sides of the pan.

  4. Add the chopped Serrano ham and season with salt, black pepper, and nutmeg to taste. Stir until well combined.

  5. Transfer the mixture to a shallow dish and let it cool to room temperature. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until the mixture is firm enough to shape into croquettes.

  6. In a shallow dish, beat the egg. In another shallow dish, place the breadcrumbs.

  7. Using a tablespoon or a cookie scoop, shape the mixture into small cylinders or balls.

  8. Dip each croquette into the beaten egg, then coat it in breadcrumbs, pressing gently to make sure the breadcrumbs adhere.

  9. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a heavy-bottomed pot until it reaches 350°F (175°C).

  10. Fry the croquettes in batches until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes per batch. Drain on paper towels.
